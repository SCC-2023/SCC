Frequently Asked Questions

Income tax is a tax charged on the annual income earned by an individual. The amount of tax paid will depend on how much money you earn as income over a financial year.
Income tax is calculated by applying the applicable tax rate to your taxable income. Taxable income is determined by subtracting allowable deductions and exemptions from your total income.
There are mainly two different types of taxes, direct taxes and indirect taxes. The implementation of both taxes is different and separate from one another. Some of them are paid directly, for example income tax, corporate tax, wealth tax, etc., while some are paid indirectly, like sales tax, service tax, value added tax, etc.
Gross income refers to the total income you earn from all sources before any deductions. Taxable income, on the other hand, is the income amount on which you are required to pay taxes after subtracting allowable deductions and exemptions.
Tax deductions and exemptions are certain expenses or allowances that can be subtracted from your taxable income, reducing the amount of income subject to taxation. Examples include mortgage interest, medical expenses, and certain contributions to retirement plans.
A tax bracket is a range of income levels that are subject to a specific tax rate. Tax rates increase as you move up to higher income brackets. Taxpayers are typically taxed at a progressive rate, meaning they pay a higher percentage of tax on higher portions of their income.
Generally, individuals and non-audit cases must file their income tax returns by 31 July and audit cases by 31 October.
Paying your taxes before the end of the financial year is called an advance tax or pay-as-you-earn scheme. You have to pay this tax if you owe more than Rs. 10,000 in taxes for the year. You can pay it in instalments on specific dates, and it needs to be paid in the same year you earned your income. This system helps the government have a steady income flow throughout the year.
